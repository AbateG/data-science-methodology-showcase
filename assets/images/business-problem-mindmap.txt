# Business Problem Mind Map Placeholder

## Recommended Content:

A mind map visualizing the credit card customer churn business problem with the following structure:

**Center Node: Credit Card Customer Churn**

**Main Branches:**

1. **Business Context**
   - Mid-sized credit card issuer
   - Competitive market
   - Customer acquisition costs 5-7x retention costs
   - Revenue from existing customers critical

2. **Core Problem**
   - Reduce churn by 15% (from 18% to 15.3%)
   - Increase cross-sell revenue by 20%
   - Improve CLV by 12%

3. **Business Objectives**
   - Financial: $24.3M annual net benefit
   - Operational: Proactive retention strategies
   - Strategic: Data-driven customer management

4. **Success Criteria**
   - Technical: AUC > 0.85, Precision@10% > 0.60
   - Business: ROI > 5:1, measurable impact
   - Operational: Production deployment, monitoring

5. **Stakeholders**
   - Client: Head of Customer Retention (CMO)
   - Data Scientist: Lead analyst
   - Users: Retention team, marketing team
   - Partners: IT, data engineering, compliance

6. **Data Sources**
   - Internal: CBS, transactions, CRM, digital engagement
   - External: Credit bureau data
   - Volume: 500K customers, 50M transactions/month

7. **Key Challenges**
   - Class imbalance (18% churn)
   - Data quality issues (missing income, etc.)
   - Time-based prediction (prevent data leakage)
   - Interpretability requirements
   - Production deployment complexity

8. **Solution Approach**
   - CRISP-DM methodology
   - XGBoost + ensemble models
   - Feature engineering (RFM, trends, velocity)
   - SMOTE for class balancing
   - Time-based validation
   - Phased deployment

9. **Expected Outcomes**
   - Churn prediction model (AUC 0.89)
   - Cross-sell recommendations (Precision@5 0.39)
   - Customer segmentation (5 actionable groups)
   - Production system with monitoring
   - 9.1:1 ROI

10. **Risks & Mitigations**
    - Model degradation: Continuous monitoring
    - Implementation failure: Phased rollout
    - Ethical concerns: Fairness audits
    - Resource constraints: Prioritized scope
